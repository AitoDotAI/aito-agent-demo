aito..Executive brief · for teams building LLM agents on their own data

Your agents reason brilliantly — and forget your business. Give them intuition.

You can't feasibly train a foundation model on your ERP, and RAG only bolts memory on beside a frozen, internet-trained mind. Aito gives your agents a calibrated intuition for your own data — the same act as a neural network (pattern → instant answer), but live, memory-native, and yours. No training. No MLOps. Every answer auditable.

The faculty your stack is missing
The LLM
Reasoning
General, deliberate thinking — but a frozen intuition about the whole internet, with nothing learned about your customers, codes, or history.
RAG / vector store
Memory
Recall of what was stored — copied into the prompt to be re-read and re-reasoned every call. Useful, but separate from the intuition.
Aito
Intuition
Pattern → instant, calibrated answer, from your data directly. Live (a row added today is in the next prediction). Tells you how sure it is.
It fits your platform — it doesn't compete with it
Aito has no agents, no orchestrator, no UI to defend. It's a single query — _predict, _match, _relate, _estimate, _recommend — your agents call like a tool or MCP endpoint. Your platform stays the brain; Aito is the instant, calibrated memory underneath it. Keep your model, keep your orchestration; just stop asking the LLM to do the structured, numeric, high-volume recall it's bad at.
